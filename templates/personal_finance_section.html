About the 50/30/20 Rule

The 50/30/20 rule is a budgeting method that suggests allocating 50% of your income to necessities, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings and debt repayment.

About the Rule of 25

The Rule of 25 is a retirement planning rule that estimates how much you need to save to cover your annual expenses. It suggests that you need to save 25 times your annual expenses to achieve financial independence.

About the 70:20:10 Budgeting Rule

The 70:20:10 budgeting rule recommends allocating 70% of your income to living expenses and necessities, 20% to savings, investments, and debt reduction, and 10% to discretionary spending and personal enjoyment.
